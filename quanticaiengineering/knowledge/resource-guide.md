# Resource Guide — Advanced Players (Level 45+)

> **Scope caveat:** Most non-daily resources in Isekai: Slow Life come from **events**, which cycle in and out on a schedule. This doc keeps resource sourcing at a high level — "where this resource comes from broadly" — rather than listing exact per-event drop rates and prices. Specific event integration is a future audit pass; until then, assume event-based sources vary based on whether the relevant event is currently running.

## The Six Broad Resource Sources

For any resource in the game, its primary pathways are some combination of:

1. **Stages (Adventure progression)** — clearing stages gives EXP items, basic resources, and unlocks. Stage clear rate is **capped by your fellow power** — the more Fellow Power you have, the more stages you can complete, which in turn gives you more of nearly every basic resource. This makes fellow power a multiplier on every other resource income stream.
2. **Event shops** — every time an event runs, its shop contains that event's specific currency exchanges. Most non-daily resources (skill pearls, crystal ores, magic ores, acquaint stones, advanced bait, fellow fragments) have an event shop as the highest-income source when the right event is running.
3. **In-game shops (F2P-friendly)** — regular permanent shops that refresh daily/weekly/monthly and don't require event participation. Crystals spend here goes fairly far.
4. **Expo gacha** — event-adjacent gacha pulls that drop resources. Usually tied to current events but with a permanent baseline.
5. **Other gacha + passes** — Fountain of Wishes, Battle Pass, premium passes, character gacha all drop resources as secondary rewards on top of their primary prize.
6. **Daily activities** — guild donations, random requests, school/inn tasks, fellow diary, archdemon challenge. Small per-day but compound over weeks.

## Crystals — The Universal High-Value Currency

Crystals deserve a separate section because they're the most broadly-usable currency in the game and should never be burned on niche purchases.

### Where Crystals Come From
- **Daily character income** — based on your account level, your character gives you a fixed number of crystals per day. Higher account level = more daily crystals.
- **Event rewards** — Fellow Diary, School Task, Inn Task, Archdemon Challenge, and many other recurring event-adjacent activities all drop crystals.
- **Level-up events** — hitting account milestones
- **Achievements and one-time rewards**
- **Real-money purchases** — buying crystal packs directly, or getting crystals as a side reward when you buy other items with real money (these purchases also give VIP points and additional items)

### What Crystals Are Best Spent On
- **Event stamina items** — during a limited event, crystals can convert to more stamina/tickets/tries at that event, accelerating your event progression
- **Shop items like White Pearls and Black Pearls** — the best crystal sink for endgame optimization (White Pearls → bait production speed, Black Pearls → account-wide % boosts; see `pearls.md`)
- **Fancy shop offers** — higher-tier premium shop items that have broad utility
- **Specific event-exclusive items** where nothing else in the economy gives a substitute

### What NOT To Spend Crystals On
- **Dedicated dates** — dating is better served by Succubus Tonic (abundant), Plane Tickets (rare but targeted), or Airship Journey (event-sourced). Crystal-gated dates exist but are wasteful compared to other crystal uses.
- **Impulse building gold top-ups** — gold is abundant, crystals are scarce; don't convert down
- **Common resources already available from events** — wait for the event and save crystals for scarce items

### VIP Points From Real-Money Spending
When you spend real money in the game, on top of whatever you bought, you also accumulate **VIP points**. VIP points unlock progressive perks at thresholds (VIP 1, 2, 3, ... through VIP 8 and beyond). Higher VIP tiers unlock specific fellows (Neptune is VIP 8, Shlomo is VIP 7, Mammon is VIP 2, Trady is VIP 4, Mescal is VIP 5) and various account bonuses. Details on VIP benefits TBD.

## Acquaint Stones (Awakening)

| Source | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Event commissions / rankings | Can give up to ~35 stones per event cycle |
| Event gacha | Chance per draw (~2.5% base probability) |
| Fountain of Wishes | Low-rate random drop of full stones |
| Synthesizing stone fragments | **20 fragments → 1 stone** via crafting — steady source from event fragment drops |
| Event shops and ranking rewards | Varies per event |

**Priority order:** Event commissions > Synthesizing fragments > Event gacha > Fountain of Wishes. **Acquaint Stones are one of the rarest resources in the game — do not waste them on fellows you won't main.**

## Skill Pearls

Skill Pearls are type-agnostic and feed many different aptitude slots (see `skill-pearls.md`). Sources:

| Source | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Event rewards | Main source — varies by event |
| Fishing event rankings | Top placements drop substantial skill pearl rewards |
| Event shops | Skill pearls are commonly stocked in event shops |
| Battle Pass | Per-season drops |
| In-game shops | Occasional stock |
| Crafting | Variable |

**Priority order:** Event rewards + rankings > Event shops > Battle Pass > In-game shops. Plan around current events for skill pearl farming.

## Crystal Ores (Limit Breaks)

Used for pushing fellow level caps (Lv 150-550 tier). Sources:

| Source | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Guild Exchange Shop | Limited daily purchases — reliable baseline |
| Event shops | Variable stock per event |
| Adventure stage drops | Passive drops while clearing stages |
| Event rewards / rankings | Event-gated |

**Priority order:** Guild Exchange (daily) > Event shops > Adventure stages. Save for your main carry first.

## Magic Ore (Artifacts)

Used for leveling artifacts (70 ores per level, constant) and materia (40 ores per level, constant). Sources:

| Source | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Adventure stages | Main passive source |
| Event rewards | Usually substantial per event |
| Guild Exchange | Limited daily |

**Reminder:** Breakthrough Materia (needed to push materia past Lv 10) is a separate scarce resource, NOT Magic Ore. See `artifacts.md`.

## Blessing Points (Family Blessings)

Blessing Points are earned PER FAMILY MEMBER by dating them. See `family-blessings.md` for the full mechanics. Quick reference on date types:

| Date Type | Cost | Target Selection | Notes |
|-----------|------|------------------|-------|
| **Succubus Tonic** (3 date chances per tonic) | Succubus Tonics (abundant) | Random — RNG picks the family | Bulk BP farming across your roster |
| **Plane Tickets** | Plane Tickets (hard to come by) | You pick the family | 2 guaranteed children per trip — best for School task |
| **Crystal Travel** | Crystals (see Crystals section — generally NOT recommended unless you're crystal-rich) | You pick the family | 1 guaranteed child per trip, escalating cost per click |
| **Sailing Trip** | Expensive (crystals or perfume) | (See family-blessings.md) | Real system but expensive; mostly Succubus Tonic is more efficient |
| **Airship Journey** | Event-sourced | (See family-blessings.md) | Event reward items, spent mainly on the School task |

**Practical rule:** Use Succubus Tonic as your daily bulk dating source. Burn Plane Tickets when you have them (scarce but powerful). Save crystals for higher-ROI uses; crystal travel is only worth it if you specifically need a child encounter on a family Plane Tickets won't cover.

## Character Fragments (Stella)

| Source | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Duplicate gacha pulls | Each dupe of a specific character gives that character's fragments |
| Event shops | Event-specific fellow fragments |
| Event rankings | Top placements often give fragments of the event's featured fellow |
| Character shops | Dedicated shops that rotate fellow fragments |

**Reminder:** Archdemons use **Purple Stars** (shared across all Archdemons) and Otherworld Valiants use **Insignias** (shared across all Valiants). Ancient Magi use per-fellow Magi tokens (NOT shared between Magi). All other groups use per-fellow character fragments. See `stella.md`.

## Advanced Bait (Fishing)

| Source | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Daily cap of 1 free Advanced Bait** | Hard-capped — never skip your daily |
| Event shops | Occasionally stocks Advanced Bait for event currencies |

That's it — Advanced Bait is hard-capped. Use your daily, take any event shop Advanced Bait when offered, and don't expect to buy your way around the cap.

## Reforge Oils (Artifact Skill Rerolls)

| Source | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| Fountain of Wishes | Drop chance per wish |
| Event rewards | Per event |
| Adventure drops | Ongoing passive |
| In-game shops | Sometimes stocked |

**Key fact:** Reforge Oils are **widely available** from many sources, and artifact skill rerolls are unlimited as long as you have oil. This is why rerolling to target-typing skills is a normal part of artifact progression.

- [ ] Daily character crystal claim
- [ ] 4× Basic Guild Donations
- [ ] Random Requests (highest rarity available)
- [ ] Daily Acquaint Stone fragments from events (if any active)
- [ ] Use all Date Points (Succubus Tonic or targeted dates)
- [ ] Daily Advanced Bait at priority fishing spot
- [ ] Use all regenerating Normal Bait before the pool caps
- [ ] Complete daily missions for EXP Stones
- [ ] School task (field trips → blessing power + children)
- [ ] Inn task
- [ ] Archdemon Challenge
- [ ] Fellow Diary
- [ ] Collect Farmstead / Village gold income
- [ ] Check event shops for Skill Pearls and Crystal Ores
- [ ] Buy daily Guild Exchange Crystal Ores
- [ ] Clear as many adventure stages as your fellow power allows
