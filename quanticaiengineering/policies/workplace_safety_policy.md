## Policy Statement

### Commitment to Safety
Quantic AI Engineering is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees, contractors, visitors, and anyone present on company premises. Safety is everyone's responsibility, and we expect all individuals to prioritize safety in their daily activities.

### Zero Harm Goal
Our goal is zero workplace injuries and illnesses. We achieve this through:
- Proactive hazard identification and mitigation
- Comprehensive safety training
- Employee engagement and accountability
- Continuous improvement of safety practices

### Regulatory Compliance
This policy ensures compliance with:
- Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) regulations
- State and local safety codes
- Building and fire codes
- Industry best practices

### Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and vendors working on company premises
- Visitors to company facilities
- Home offices for remote workers (see Remote Work Safety section)

## Safety Responsibilities

### Management Responsibilities
**Executive Leadership:**
- Establish safety as a core company value
- Allocate resources for safety programs
- Lead by example in following safety practices
- Review safety metrics and incident reports

**Department Managers:**
- Ensure team compliance with safety policies
- Conduct regular safety discussions in team meetings
- Investigate incidents and near-misses
- Address safety concerns promptly
- Ensure employees receive required training

**Facilities Manager:**
- Maintain safe building conditions
- Conduct regular safety inspections
- Coordinate emergency drills
- Manage relationships with safety vendors
- Maintain safety equipment and systems

### Safety Committee
- Meets monthly to review safety issues
- Conducts workplace inspections
- Recommends policy improvements
- Reviews incident trends
- Plans safety awareness campaigns

**Committee Composition:**
- Facilities Manager (Chair)
- Employee representatives from each department
- HR representative
- Security representative

### Employee Responsibilities
Every employee must:
- Follow all safety policies and procedures
- Use provided safety equipment properly
- Report hazards, injuries, and near-misses immediately
- Participate in safety training
- Suggest safety improvements
- Look out for colleagues' safety

Employees have the **right to refuse unsafe work** without retaliation. If you believe a task is unsafe:
1. Stop work immediately
2. Notify your supervisor
3. Explain the safety concern
4. Work with supervisor to make task safe or find alternative approach

### Contractors and Vendors
Third parties working on company premises must:
- Comply with all company safety policies
- Provide proof of insurance and safety training
- Attend safety orientation before beginning work
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Report incidents immediately

## Workplace Safety Rules

### General Safety Rules
1. **Stay alert and focused** - No distractions in hazardous areas
2. **Keep work areas clean** - Clean up spills immediately, remove clutter
3. **Use equipment properly** - Follow manufacturer instructions
4. **Wear appropriate attire** - Closed-toe shoes required in office; no loose clothing near machinery
5. **No horseplay** - Pranks and roughhousing prohibited
6. **Report hazards** - Don't assume someone else will report it
7. **Follow signage** - Obey all safety signs and warnings
8. **Never bypass safety devices** - Guards and safety features must remain in place

### Prohibited Behaviors
- Working under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Possessing weapons on company property
- Disabling smoke detectors or fire alarms
- Blocking emergency exits or fire extinguishers
- Running in buildings (except during emergencies)
- Using damaged equipment or tools

### Office Safety

**Slip, Trip, and Fall Prevention:**
- Keep walkways clear of cords, boxes, and clutter
- Clean up spills immediately
- Report damaged flooring or carpeting
- Use handrails on stairs
- Don't walk while using mobile device
- Wear appropriate footwear (avoid slick soles)

**Lifting and Material Handling:**
- Get help for items >50 lbs
- Bend at knees, not waist
- Keep load close to body
- Don't twist while carrying
- Use carts or dollies for multiple items
- Request assistance rather than overexert

**Electrical Safety:**
- Don't overload outlets (use surge protectors)
- Keep electrical equipment away from water
- Report frayed cords or damaged plugs
- No personal space heaters (unless approved model)
- Unplug equipment before cleaning
- Keep ventilation openings clear

**Fire Safety:**
- Know location of fire extinguishers and exits
- Don't prop open fire doors
- Keep 3-foot clearance around electrical panels
- No candles or open flames
- Report smoke or burning odors immediately
- Follow evacuation procedures

### Laboratory and Equipment Safety
(For facilities with labs or specialized equipment)

**Access Control:**
- Authorized personnel only
- Badge access required
- Visitors must be escorted
- Training required before equipment use

**Personal Protective Equipment:**
- Safety glasses required in labs
- Lab coats when handling chemicals
- Gloves appropriate for materials being handled
- Hearing protection in high-noise areas

**Chemical Safety:**
- Review Safety Data Sheets (SDS) before use
- Use chemicals only in designated areas
- Proper storage (locked cabinets, segregation)
- Label all containers
- Use fume hoods when required
- Know location of eyewash and safety shower

**Equipment Safety:**
- Never operate unfamiliar equipment
- Complete required training
- Inspect equipment before use
- Report malfunctions immediately
- Lock out/tag out during maintenance
- Keep guards in place

## Hazard Communication

### Right to Know
Employees have the right to know about hazardous materials in the workplace. We comply with OSHA's Hazard Communication Standard (HazCom).

### Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
- SDS available for all hazardous materials on premises
- Located in: Safety binder in main office and lab
- Also available electronically: safety.quanticai.com/sds
- Employees must review SDS before using chemicals
- Contact Safety Committee with questions

### Chemical Labeling
All containers must be labeled with:
- Product name
- Hazard warnings
- Manufacturer information
- Pictograms (where applicable)

Never use materials from unlabeled containers.

### Training
Employees working with hazardous materials receive:
- Initial HazCom training
- Training on specific chemicals used in their area
- Annual refresher training
- Updates when new hazards introduced

## Emergency Procedures

### Emergency Contacts
- **Fire/Medical/Police Emergency:** 911
- **Building Security:** Extension 5500 or (415) 555-5500
- **Facilities Emergency:** Extension 5505 (24/7 hotline)
- **Safety Committee:** safety@quanticai.com

### Fire Emergency

**If You Discover Fire:**
1. Pull nearest fire alarm
2. Call 911 (provide address: 100 Innovation Way, San Francisco)
3. Evacuate building immediately
4. Do NOT attempt to fight large fires

**Using Fire Extinguisher (small fires only):**
Remember PASS:
- **P**ull the pin
- **A**im at base of fire
- **S**queeze handle
- **S**weep side to side

If fire doesn't go out quickly, evacuate immediately.

**Evacuation Procedures:**
1. Remain calm
2. Use nearest safe exit (stairs, not elevators)
3. Help visitors and guests
4. Assist individuals with disabilities
5. Close doors behind you (don't lock)
6. Go to designated assembly area
7. Await instructions from floor wardens
8. Do NOT re-enter building until cleared by fire department

**Assembly Areas:**
- Building A: North parking lot by flagpole
- Building B: South lawn near main entrance

**Floor Wardens:**
- Designated employees on each floor
- Identifiable by orange vest
- Ensure evacuation of floor
- Account for employees at assembly area
- Report missing individuals to incident commander

### Medical Emergency

**In Case of Injury or Illness:**
1. Call 911 for serious injuries/illnesses
2. Call Security (Ext. 5500) for non-life-threatening issues
3. Do NOT move injured person unless immediate danger
4. Render first aid only if trained
5. Stay with injured person until help arrives
6. Report all incidents (see Incident Reporting section)

**First Aid:**
- First aid kits located in break rooms and reception areas
- Automated External Defibrillators (AED) in main lobby and 3rd floor
- Trained first aiders: See list posted on safety board

### Earthquake

**During Earthquake:**
1. **Drop, Cover, Hold On**
2. Get under desk or table
3. Cover head and neck
4. Hold on until shaking stops
5. If outdoors, move away from buildings

**After Earthquake:**
1. Check for injuries
2. Evacuate if building damaged or alarm sounds
3. Use stairs (not elevators)
4. Stay alert for aftershocks
5. Do not re-enter building until cleared

### Active Shooter / Violent Intruder

**Run, Hide, Fight:**

**RUN:**
- If safe exit route available, run
- Leave belongings behind
- Help others escape if possible
- Call 911 when safe

**HIDE:**
- If escape not possible, hide
- Lock and barricade doors
- Turn off lights
- Silence phones
- Hide behind solid objects
- Remain silent

**FIGHT:**
- As last resort, if confronted
- Act with aggression
- Improvise weapons
- Commit to your actions

**When Police Arrive:**
- Keep hands visible
- Follow all commands
- Don't make sudden movements
- Provide information about threat

### Severe Weather
- Monitor weather alerts
- Follow instructions from management
- Shelter in place if tornado warning
- Interior rooms away from windows
- Basement or lowest floor preferred

### Power Outage
- Emergency lighting activates automatically
- Remain in place initially
- Listen for announcements
- Evacuate if instructed
- Use stairs, not elevators

### Emergency Drills
- Fire drills conducted quarterly
- Earthquake drills conducted twice annually
- Participation mandatory
- Treat all drills as real emergencies
- Feedback welcome to improve procedures

## Incident Reporting and Investigation

### What to Report
Report ALL of the following immediately:
- **Injuries:** Even minor cuts, sprains, bumps
- **Near-misses:** Incidents that could have caused injury
- **Property damage:** Damaged equipment, spills, breakage
- **Unsafe conditions:** Hazards, defects, malfunctions
- **Security concerns:** Suspicious activity, unauthorized access

### Reporting Timeline
- **Injuries requiring medical attention:** Immediately (within 1 hour)
- **Other injuries:** Same day
- **Near-misses and hazards:** Within 24 hours

### Reporting Methods
1. **Immediate/Serious:** Call Security (Ext. 5500) or Facilities (Ext. 5505)
2. **Written Report:** Submit Incident Report Form
   - Available on intranet: safety.quanticai.com/report
   - Paper forms at reception desk
   - Email to: safety@quanticai.com
3. **Anonymous:** Safety suggestion box in break room

### Information to Include
- Date, time, and location of incident
- Individuals involved (names, job titles)
- Witnesses
- Detailed description of what happened
- Contributing factors
- Injuries or property damage
- Immediate actions taken

### Investigation Process
All reported incidents are investigated to:
- Determine root cause
- Identify corrective actions
- Prevent recurrence
- Comply with regulatory reporting requirements

**Investigation Steps:**
1. Secure scene and preserve evidence
2. Interview involved parties and witnesses
3. Review relevant documents, procedures, and training records
4. Analyze contributing factors
5. Determine root cause
6. Develop corrective action plan
7. Document findings
8. Implement preventive measures
9. Follow up to ensure effectiveness

### Workers' Compensation
Employees injured on the job may be eligible for workers' compensation benefits:
- Medical treatment (no employee cost)
- Temporary disability payments (if unable to work)
- Permanent disability benefits (if applicable)
- Vocational rehabilitation (if needed)

**Filing a Claim:**
1. Report injury immediately to supervisor and HR
2. Seek medical treatment from approved provider (list provided by HR)
3. Complete Employee's Claim Form (DWC-1 in California)
4. HR submits claim to insurance carrier
5. Follow treating physician's instructions
6. Keep HR informed of medical status

**Return to Work:**
- Modified duty available when possible
- Work with doctor, HR, and manager on accommodations
- Goal is safe return to full duties

## Ergonomics

### Office Ergonomics

**Workstation Setup:**
- **Monitor:** Eye level, arm's length away, directly in front
- **Keyboard/Mouse:** Elbow height, wrists straight
- **Chair:** Lumbar support, feet flat on floor or footrest, thighs parallel to floor
- **Desk:** Adequate space for work materials

**Posture:**
- Sit back in chair with lumbar support
- Shoulders relaxed
- Elbows at 90-100 degrees
- Wrists neutral (not bent)
- Feet supported

**Breaks and Movement:**
- Stand or stretch every hour
- 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds
- Vary tasks to avoid prolonged repetition
- Walk during phone calls when possible

### Ergonomic Equipment
Available upon request through Facilities:
- Adjustable chairs
- Monitor stands
- Keyboard trays
- Footrests
- Sit-stand desk converters
- Ergonomic keyboards and mice
- Document holders

Submit request: facilities@quanticai.com or Ext. 5505

### Ergonomic Assessments
Employees experiencing discomfort should request an ergonomic assessment:
- Conducted by trained assessor
- Evaluates workstation setup
- Recommends adjustments and equipment
- Follow-up to ensure resolution
- Request at: safety@quanticai.com

### Reporting Discomfort Early
Don't wait for pain to become severe. Report:
- Persistent discomfort
- Numbness or tingling
- Reduced range of motion
- Symptoms that worsen with work
Early intervention prevents serious injury.

## Remote Work Safety

### Home Office Safety
Remote employees responsible for maintaining safe home workspace.

**Workspace Requirements:**
- Adequate lighting
- Stable work surface
- Ergonomic chair
- Free from tripping hazards
- Electrical outlets not overloaded
- Smoke detectors functional

**Ergonomics:**
- Follow office ergonomics guidelines above
- Request ergonomic equipment through IT or Facilities
- Take regular breaks and stretch

**Safety Considerations:**
- Electrical cords secured and not frayed
- Fire extinguisher accessible
- Emergency exits clear
- Keep workspace clean and organized

### Reporting Home Office Injuries
Injuries occurring during remote work hours in home office may be covered by workers' compensation:
- Report immediately to supervisor and HR
- Complete incident report
- Provide details of circumstances
- Workers' comp evaluation determines coverage

### Video Call Backgrounds
- Ensure safe, professional background
- Avoid clutter or hazards visible in camera view
- Adequate lighting for video calls

### Mental Health and Well-being
Remote work can present unique challenges:
- Maintain work-life boundaries
- Take regular breaks
- Stay connected with colleagues
- Seek support if feeling isolated
- Use Employee Assistance Program (EAP) resources

## Policy Enforcement

### Compliance Expectations
All employees expected to comply with safety policies. Noncompliance puts everyone at risk.

### Progressive Discipline
Safety violations may result in:
1. Verbal warning and coaching
2. Written warning
3. Suspension
4. Termination

Serious or willful violations may result in immediate termination.

### No Retaliation
Employees will not face retaliation for:
- Reporting safety concerns
- Filing injury reports
- Refusing unsafe work
- Participating in safety investigations
- Requesting ergonomic accommodations

Retaliation is grounds for disciplinary action.
