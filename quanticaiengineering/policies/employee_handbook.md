## Employment Basics

### Employment Classification

**Full-Time Employees:** Work 40 hours per week and are eligible for full benefits including health insurance, retirement plans, and paid time off.

**Part-Time Employees:** Work less than 30 hours per week and receive prorated benefits based on hours worked.

**Contract Workers:** Engaged for specific projects or time periods. Benefits eligibility varies by contract terms.

**Interns:** Temporary positions for students or recent graduates. May be paid or unpaid depending on program.

### Probationary Period
All new employees undergo a 90-day probationary period during which performance and fit are evaluated. During this time, employment may be terminated by either party with minimal notice.

### Work Hours and Schedules
Standard business hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday. However, we offer flexible scheduling options in many departments. Employees should coordinate with their managers to establish appropriate work schedules.

### Attendance and Punctuality
Regular attendance and punctuality are essential. Employees should notify their supervisor as soon as possible if they will be late or absent. Excessive absenteeism or tardiness may result in disciplinary action.

### Timekeeping Requirements
Non-exempt employees must accurately record all hours worked using our electronic timekeeping system. Falsifying time records is grounds for immediate termination.

## Code of Conduct

### Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect and professionalism
- Maintain honest and ethical business practices
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Protect company confidential information
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations

### Dress Code
Business casual attire is appropriate for most positions. Employees meeting with clients should dress in business professional attire. Department-specific requirements may apply.

### Substance Abuse
The use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs on company premises is strictly prohibited. Reporting to work under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs is cause for immediate termination.

### Violence and Weapons
We maintain a zero-tolerance policy regarding workplace violence. Weapons of any kind are prohibited on company property, including parking facilities.

## Workplace Standards

### Communication Standards
- Use professional language in all business communications
- Respond to emails and messages within 24 business hours
- Keep confidential information secure
- Avoid gossip and unprofessional discussions

- Arrive on time and prepared
- Mute devices during meetings

### Workspace Maintenance
Employees are responsible for keeping their work areas clean, organized, and safe. Report any maintenance issues to facilities immediately.

## Anti-Discrimination and Harassment

### Equal Employment Opportunity
Quantic AI Engineering is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic.

### Harassment Policy
Harassment of any kind is strictly prohibited. This includes:
- Verbal harassment (derogatory comments, slurs, jokes)
- Physical harassment (unwanted touching, assault)
- Visual harassment (offensive images, gestures)
- Sexual harassment (unwanted advances, requests for favors)

### Reporting Procedures
Employees who experience or witness discrimination or harassment should report it immediately to:
1. Their direct supervisor
2. Human Resources Department
3. Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS

All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially. Retaliation against those who report concerns is strictly prohibited.

## Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. This includes:

### Outside Employment
Employees may engage in outside employment if it does not interfere with their duties or create a conflict of interest. Prior written approval from HR is required.

### Financial Interests
Employees should not have significant financial interests in competitors, suppliers, or customers without disclosure and approval.

### Gifts and Entertainment
Employees may not accept gifts or entertainment from vendors or customers that could influence business decisions. Nominal gifts under $50 are generally acceptable.

### Family Relationships
Employees in supervisory positions may not directly manage family members or individuals with whom they have a close personal relationship.

## Termination and Resignation

### Voluntary Resignation
Employees who wish to resign should provide at least two weeks' written notice. Senior staff and managers should provide four weeks' notice when possible.

### Involuntary Termination
Employment may be terminated for various reasons including:
- Poor performance
- Violation of company policies
- Misconduct
- Business necessity or restructuring

### Exit Procedures
Upon termination or resignation, employees must:
- Return all company property (laptop, phone, keys, badges)
- Complete exit interview with HR
- Receive final paycheck including accrued vacation (per state law)
- Review continuation of benefits options (COBRA)

### References
HR will verify dates of employment and job title only. Direct supervisors may provide references at their discretion.
