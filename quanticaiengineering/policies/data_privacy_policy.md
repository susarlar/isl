## Policy Statement

### Purpose
Quantic AI Engineering is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of our employees, customers, partners, and other individuals whose information we process. This policy establishes requirements for collecting, using, storing, and disposing of personal data in compliance with applicable privacy laws and regulations.

### Regulatory Framework
This policy ensures compliance with:
- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** - European Union
- **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)** and **California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)**
- **Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)** - Canada
- **UK Data Protection Act 2018**
- **Other applicable state, federal, and international privacy laws**

### Scope
This policy applies to all personal data processed by Quantic AI Engineering, including:
- Employee and applicant data
- Customer and client data
- Website visitor data
- Vendor and business partner data
- Marketing and sales data
- AI training data containing personal information

### Roles and Responsibilities

**Chief Privacy Officer (CPO):**
- Oversees privacy program
- Ensures policy compliance
- Serves as point of contact for regulators
- Reviews privacy incidents

**Data Protection Officer (DPO):**
- Monitors GDPR compliance
- Advises on data protection impact assessments
- Cooperates with supervisory authorities
- Contact: dpo@quanticai.com

**Department Managers:**
- Ensure team compliance with privacy policy
- Approve data processing activities in their area
- Escalate privacy concerns to CPO

**All Employees:**
- Comply with privacy policy
- Complete privacy training
- Report privacy incidents promptly
- Handle personal data responsibly

## Privacy Principles

### Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
Personal data must be:
- Processed lawfully based on valid legal basis
- Collected and used in ways that are fair and not misleading
- Processed transparently with clear notice to individuals

**Valid Legal Bases for Processing:**
1. **Consent:** Individual has given clear affirmative consent
2. **Contract:** Processing necessary to fulfill contract with individual
3. **Legal Obligation:** Processing required by law
4. **Vital Interests:** Processing necessary to protect someone's life
5. **Public Interest:** Processing necessary for public interest task
6. **Legitimate Interest:** Processing necessary for legitimate business interests (with balancing test)

### Purpose Limitation
- Personal data collected only for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes
- Data not processed in manner incompatible with original purpose
- New purposes require new legal basis or consent
- Purpose clearly stated in privacy notice

### Data Minimization
- Collect only data that is adequate, relevant, and necessary
- Avoid "nice to have" data collection
- Regularly review data collection forms and processes
- Delete unnecessary data fields

### Accuracy
- Take reasonable steps to ensure data accuracy
- Update data when notified of inaccuracies
- Provide mechanisms for individuals to update their information
- Verify data accuracy before making important decisions

### Storage Limitation
- Retain data only as long as necessary for stated purpose
- Define retention periods for each data category
- Securely delete data when retention period expires
- Archive data appropriately when legally required to retain

### Integrity and Confidentiality
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Protect against unauthorized processing, loss, or damage
- Regular security testing and monitoring
- Incident response procedures in place

### Accountability
- Demonstrate compliance with privacy principles
- Maintain documentation of processing activities
- Conduct regular privacy audits
- Implement privacy by design and by default

- Contact information (name, address, phone, email)
- Employment details (job title, department, hire date, salary)

- Referral source
- Geographic location (country/city level)

**Marketing Data:**
- Email address and contact preferences

Personal data may be collected through:
- Online forms and applications

Before collecting personal data, provide clear privacy notice including:
- Identity and contact details of data controller
- Purpose of processing and legal basis
- Categories of personal data collected
- Recipients or categories of recipients
- International transfer information (if applicable)
- Retention period or criteria

Privacy notices must be:
- Concise, transparent, intelligible
- Written in clear and plain language
- Easily accessible
- Provided at time of data collection
- Updated when processing changes

## Data Subject Rights

Individuals have the following rights regarding their personal data:

### Right to Access
- Individuals can request copy of their personal data
- Respond within 30 days (may extend to 60 days for complex requests)
- Provide information free of charge
- Verify identity before providing data
- Process requests via email to privacy@quanticai.com

**Information to Provide:**
- Categories of data processed
- Purposes of processing
- Recipients of data
- Retention period
- Source of data (if not collected directly)
- Existence of automated decision-making

### Right to Rectification
- Individuals can request correction of inaccurate data
- Update data within 30 days
- Notify recipients of data if rectification affects them
- Allow direct updates through self-service portals when possible

### Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")
Individuals can request deletion when:
- Data no longer necessary for original purpose
- Individual withdraws consent
- Individual objects to processing
- Data processed unlawfully
- Legal obligation requires erasure
- Data collected from children

**Exceptions - Cannot Erase When:**
- Compliance with legal obligation
- Exercise or defense of legal claims
- Public interest or official authority
- Establishment of legal claims

### Right to Restrict Processing
- Individual can request limitation of processing
- Mark records as restricted; do not process except for storage
- Notify individual before lifting restriction
- Inform recipients of restriction

### Right to Data Portability
- Provide data in structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (CSV, JSON)
- Applies to data processed based on consent or contract
- Applies to automated processing only
- Transfer directly to another controller when technically feasible

### Right to Object
- Individuals can object to processing based on legitimate interests
- Must cease processing unless compelling legitimate grounds override
- Absolute right to object to direct marketing
- Objection to automated decision-making and profiling

### Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making
- Not subject to solely automated decisions with legal/significant effect
- Right to human intervention for automated decisions
- Right to express point of view and contest decision
- Explain logic involved in automated decision-making

### Processing Data Subject Requests

**Request Submission:**
- Email: privacy@quanticai.com
- Web form: www.quanticai.com/privacy-request
- Mail: Privacy Office, Quantic AI Engineering, 100 Innovation Way, San Francisco, CA 94105

**Verification:**
- Verify identity using two forms of identification
- Additional verification for sensitive requests
- Cannot fulfill request without proper verification

**Response Timeline:**
- Respond within 30 days of receipt
- Extend up to 60 additional days for complex requests
- Notify individual of extension and reason within 30 days

**No Fee Policy:**
- Requests fulfilled free of charge
- May charge reasonable fee for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests
- May refuse clearly unfounded or excessive requests

## Data Processing Activities

### Record of Processing Activities (ROPA)
Maintain comprehensive record of all processing activities including:
- Name and contact details of controller
- Purpose of processing
- Categories of data subjects and personal data
- Categories of recipients
- International transfers and safeguards
- Retention periods
- Security measures

ROPA reviewed and updated quarterly.

### Data Processing Agreements
Execute Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with all processors who:
- Access personal data on our behalf
- Provide services involving personal data
- Are located in third-party countries

**DPA Must Include:**
- Subject matter and duration of processing
- Nature and purpose of processing
- Type of personal data and categories of data subjects
- Controller and processor obligations
- Security measures
- Sub-processor provisions
- Data subject rights assistance
- Audit rights
- Data breach notification requirements
- Post-termination data return or deletion

### Vendor Management
Before engaging vendor with access to personal data:
- Conduct privacy assessment
- Review vendor's privacy and security practices
- Execute appropriate agreements (DPA, BAA, etc.)
- Verify compliance with applicable laws
- Conduct periodic audits
- Maintain vendor inventory

### Employee Access to Personal Data
- Access granted based on need-to-know and role
- Confidentiality agreements signed
- Privacy training completed
- Access logged and monitored
- Access removed upon role change or termination

## International Data Transfers

### Transfer Mechanisms
When transferring personal data outside the originating jurisdiction:

**Adequacy Decisions:**
- Transfer to countries with adequacy decision (e.g., EU adequacy decisions)
- No additional safeguards required
- Monitor for changes to adequacy status

**Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):**
- Use EU or UK Standard Contractual Clauses
- Conduct Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA)
- Implement supplementary measures if needed
- Document and maintain signed SCCs

**Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs):**
- Internal policies for intra-group transfers
- Approved by supervisory authority
- Enforceable rights for data subjects

**Consent:**
- Obtain explicit, informed consent for transfer
- Explain risks of transfer
- Last resort when other mechanisms unavailable

**Other Derogations:**
- Performance of contract with data subject
- Important reasons of public interest
- Establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims

### Transfer Impact Assessments
Before transferring data to third countries:
- Assess laws and practices in destination country
- Evaluate risks to data subjects
- Implement supplementary measures if needed (encryption, pseudonymization)
- Document assessment and decision

### Specific Requirements

**GDPR Transfers from EU:**
- Valid transfer mechanism required for all non-adequate countries
- Special attention to US transfers post-Schrems II
- Consider data localization options

**CPRA Requirements:**
- Provide notice of transfers to third parties
- Honor opt-out requests for selling/sharing
- Execute appropriate agreements

## Data Retention

### Retention Principles
- Retain data only as long as necessary for stated purpose
- Define retention periods based on legal, regulatory, and business requirements
- Securely dispose of data after retention period
- Review and update retention schedule annually

### Retention Schedule

**Employee Records:**
- Personnel files: 7 years after separation
- Payroll records: 7 years
- Benefits records: 7 years
- I-9 forms: 3 years after hire or 1 year after termination (whichever is later)
- Job applications (not hired): 1 year
- Performance reviews: 5 years after separation
- Medical records: 30 years after termination

**Customer/Client Records:**
- Active customer records: Duration of relationship + 7 years
- Transaction records: 7 years
- Contracts: Duration + 7 years
- Marketing consent: Until withdrawn or 3 years of inactivity

**Website Data:**
- Log files: 1 year
- Cookie data: Per cookie policy (13 months maximum for non-essential)
- Analytics data: 26 months (Google Analytics default)

**Communications:**
- Email: 3 years unless specific business need
- Chat logs: 1 year
- Recorded calls: 90 days unless dispute or specific requirement

### Secure Disposal
When retention period expires:
- Automated deletion where possible
- Manual review for legal hold or other requirements
- Secure deletion methods (per Information Security Policy)
- Destruction certificates for physical media
- Notify processors to delete data

### Legal Holds
- Suspend deletion when litigation anticipated or active
- Legal team manages legal hold process
- Document scope and duration of hold
- Resume normal retention schedule when hold lifted

## Privacy by Design

### Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs)
Conduct PIA for new projects, systems, or processes that:
- Use new technologies
- Involve profiling or automated decision-making
- Process sensitive data at scale
- Monitor public areas systematically
- Could result in high risk to individuals

**PIA Process:**
1. Describe processing and purpose
2. Assess necessity and proportionality
3. Identify and evaluate risks to individuals
4. Determine measures to mitigate risks
5. Document and obtain approval from CPO
6. Review periodically and when changes occur

### Default Privacy Settings
- Design systems with privacy-protective defaults
- Opt-in (not opt-out) for non-essential processing
- Minimal data collection by default
- Shortest retention period by default
- Privacy-friendly settings pre-selected

### Data Protection by Design
Integrate privacy into system development:
- Pseudonymization and encryption by default
- Privacy requirements in design specifications
- Privacy review before production deployment
- Security testing including privacy aspects
- User-friendly privacy controls

### AI and Machine Learning
Special considerations for AI systems:
- Assess fairness and bias in algorithms
- Provide transparency about automated decisions
- Enable human review of automated decisions
- Use privacy-preserving techniques (differential privacy, federated learning)
- Avoid sensitive attributes as inputs unless justified
- Document AI model decisions and reasoning

## Data Breach Response

### Definition of Personal Data Breach
Breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to personal data.

**Types of Breaches:**
- **Confidentiality breach:** Unauthorized disclosure or access
- **Integrity breach:** Unauthorized alteration
- **Availability breach:** Loss or destruction

### Breach Detection and Reporting
Employees must immediately report suspected breaches to:
- Security Operations Center: security@quanticai.com / 1-800-555-SECURE
- Privacy Office: privacy@quanticai.com

Do not delay reporting to investigate or confirm breach.

### Breach Response Process

**1. Containment (0-4 hours):**
- Isolate affected systems
- Prevent further unauthorized access
- Preserve evidence

**2. Assessment (4-24 hours):**
- Determine scope: what data, how many individuals
- Assess severity and risk to individuals
- Identify cause and responsible parties
- Determine if breach reportable under applicable laws

**3. Notification:**

**Supervisory Authority Notification (GDPR):**
- Notify lead supervisory authority within 72 hours
- Include nature of breach, categories and approximate numbers affected, likely consequences, measures taken/proposed
- If notification delayed, provide reasons

**Individual Notification:**
- Notify affected individuals without undue delay if high risk
- Describe breach in clear, plain language
- Provide contact point for questions
- Recommend steps individuals can take
- Describe company's response

**Other Notifications:**
- State attorneys general (CCPA and state laws)
- Consumer reporting agencies (if >1,000 affected)
- Media (if extensive numbers affected)
- Business partners/clients per contract

**4. Remediation:**
- Address vulnerabilities
- Implement additional safeguards
- Offer credit monitoring if appropriate
- Document all steps taken

**5. Post-Incident Review:**
- Conduct lessons learned meeting
- Update policies and procedures
- Provide additional training
- Document incident in breach register

### Breach Documentation
Maintain record of all breaches including:
- Facts of breach
- Effects of breach
- Remedial action taken
- Assessment of whether notification required
- Notifications made and to whom
