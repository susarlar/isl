## Policy Overview

### Purpose
Quantic AI Engineering is committed to supporting employee growth and development. This policy provides guidelines for professional development activities and establishes funding available for learning opportunities.

### Philosophy
We believe that investing in employee development:
- Enhances individual capabilities and career growth
- Strengthens organizational expertise
- Improves employee engagement and retention
- Keeps our team current with industry trends and technologies

### Scope
This policy applies to all regular full-time employees who have completed their probationary period (90 days). Part-time employees may be eligible for prorated benefits.

## Professional Development Budget

### Annual Allocation

**Individual Learning Budget:**
| Position Level | Annual Budget |
|---------------|---------------|
| Individual Contributor (IC1-IC3) | $2,000 |
| Senior IC / Team Lead (IC4-IC5) | $3,000 |
| Manager | $3,500 |
| Senior Manager / Director | $5,000 |
| VP and above | $7,500 |

### Budget Guidelines
- Fiscal year runs January 1 - December 31
- Budget does not carry over to next year
- Unused budget cannot be cashed out
- Prorated for new hires based on start date
- Budget refreshes annually regardless of previous year usage

### Additional Funding
Requests exceeding annual allocation may be considered for:
- Strategic company initiatives
- Mission-critical certifications
- High-value opportunities with exceptional ROI
- Requires VP approval and special budget allocation

## Conference and Event Attendance

### Selection Criteria
When selecting conferences, consider:
- Relevance to current role and responsibilities
- Alignment with company strategic priorities
- Quality and reputation of event
- Learning objectives and expected outcomes
- Cost relative to value (ROI)
- Networking opportunities

### Popular Conferences (Examples)
- **AI/ML:** NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR, AI Summit
- **Engineering:** QCon, GitHub Universe, Cloud Native Con
- **Security:** RSA Conference, Black Hat, DEF CON
- **Leadership:** Leadership Summit, Management 3.0
- **Industry:** AWS re:Invent, Microsoft Build, Google I/O

### Approval Requirements
- Submit request at least 60 days before event
- Include conference agenda and sessions of interest
- Explain business value and learning objectives
- Identify how knowledge will be shared with team

### Covered Costs
- Conference registration fee
- Travel expenses (per Expense Reimbursement Policy)
- Hotel accommodations
- Meals (per diem rates)
- Ground transportation

### Attendance Expectations
- Attend full duration of conference
- Participate actively in sessions and workshops
- Network with peers and industry professionals
- Take notes and collect resources

### Knowledge Sharing
After attending conference, employee must:
- Present key learnings to team (lunch & learn or team meeting)
- Share resources, materials, and contacts
- Write brief summary for company knowledge base
- Due within 2 weeks of return

### Speaking Engagements
Employees invited to speak at conferences:
- Must obtain approval from manager and Marketing
- Company covers all costs
- Does not count against individual learning budget
- Encouraged to represent company and share expertise

## Tuition Reimbursement

### Program Overview
Company reimburses tuition costs for degree programs that enhance job performance and prepare employees for advancement.

### Eligibility Requirements
- Completed 1 year of continuous employment
- Current satisfactory performance rating (meets expectations or above)
- No active performance improvement plan
- Program must be job-related
- Attend accredited institution

### Eligible Degree Programs
- Undergraduate degrees (Bachelor's)
- Graduate degrees (Master's, MBA)
- Doctoral degrees (PhD)
- Certificate programs from accredited institutions

**Job-Related Requirement:**
Program must be relevant to:
- Current position responsibilities
- Logical career path within company
- Company strategic needs and priorities

### Reimbursement Amounts

| Degree Program | Annual Maximum | Per Course Maximum | Grade Requirement |
|----------------|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Undergraduate | $5,000 | $2,000 | C or higher |
| Master's / MBA | $10,000 | $3,500 | B or higher |
| Doctoral (PhD) | $15,000 | $5,000 | B or higher |

### Covered Costs
- Tuition and mandatory fees
- Required textbooks (with receipts)
- Lab fees (if required)

### NOT Covered
- Application fees
- Parking and transportation
- Meals
- Computer or equipment purchases
- Optional materials or supplies

### Application Process
1. **Pre-Approval (before enrolling):**
   - Complete Tuition Reimbursement Application
   - Attach course catalog/description
   - Explain job relevance
   - Obtain manager approval
   - Submit to HR for final approval

2. **Reimbursement (after course completion):**
   - Submit official grade report/transcript
   - Submit itemized tuition bill showing payment
   - Submit receipts for textbooks
   - Complete reimbursement request form
   - Processing within 30 days

### Grade Requirements
- Reimbursement contingent on achieving minimum grade
- Incomplete courses not reimbursed
- Failing grades not reimbursed
- Employee may retake failed course; second attempt eligible for reimbursement

### Work Schedule Accommodations
- Employees should schedule classes outside work hours when possible
- Occasional flexibility for daytime classes considered
- Flexible schedule requires manager approval
- Must meet work commitments and deadlines
- Remote work arrangements may help accommodate schedule

### Service Commitment
Due to substantial investment, service commitment required:

| Reimbursement Amount | Service Commitment |
|----------------------|--------------------|
| Up to $5,000 | 1 year after completion |
| $5,001 - $15,000 | 2 years after completion |
| Over $15,000 | 3 years after completion |

**Repayment Terms:**
- If employee voluntarily terminates during commitment period, must repay prorated amount
- Calculation: (Amount Reimbursed) × (Months Remaining / Total Commitment Months)
- Repayment deducted from final paycheck where legally permitted
- Payment plan available if balance exceeds final pay
- Waived if termination involuntary (layoff, position elimination)

## Certifications and Licenses

### Professional Certifications

**Highly Encouraged Certifications:**
- Project Management Professional (PMP)
- Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)
- AWS Certified Solutions Architect
- Google Cloud Professional Certifications
- Certified ScrumMaster (CSM)
- Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA)

### Full Reimbursement
Company provides 100% reimbursement for certifications that:
- Are required for job function
- Align with strategic company initiatives
- Provide significant value to organization

### Covered Costs
- Exam fees
- Study materials (books, practice exams)
- Preparation courses
- Renewal fees (if maintaining certification required for role)

### Study Time
- Employees expected to study outside work hours
- Some study time during work hours may be permitted with manager approval
- Study groups and peer preparation encouraged

### Exam Attempts
- First attempt: Fully covered
- Second attempt: 50% covered
- Third attempt: Employee responsible for cost

### Failure to Maintain
If certification required for position and employee fails to maintain:
- Company covers one renewal attempt
- Employee responsible for subsequent attempts
- Failure to renew may affect job responsibilities or performance rating

### License Reimbursement
Professional licenses required for position (e.g., Professional Engineer, CPA):
- Initial licensing costs covered
- Annual renewal fees covered
- Continuing education requirements covered
- Must remain in good standing

## Application and Approval Process

### Requesting Professional Development

**Step 1: Planning Discussion**
- Discuss learning goals with manager during 1:1
- Align development plans with career aspirations and business needs
- Identify specific opportunities

**Step 2: Formal Request**
- Complete Professional Development Request Form in Workday
- Include:
  - Description of activity/program
  - Cost breakdown
  - Business justification and learning objectives
  - Dates and time commitment
  - How knowledge will be applied and shared
- Attach supporting documents (course descriptions, conference agenda, etc.)

**Step 3: Approval**
- Manager reviews and approves
- HR reviews for policy compliance
- Automatically approved if within budget and meets policy criteria
- Requests exceeding budget require additional approval

**Step 4: Participation**
- Receive approval confirmation
- Register for activity (save receipts)
- Complete activity
- For conferences: Share learnings with team

**Step 5: Reimbursement**
- Submit expense report with receipts
- For tuition: Submit grades and proof of payment
- Reimbursement processed per Expense Policy timelines

### Approval Criteria
Requests evaluated based on:
- Relevance to current role or career development
- Alignment with company goals and priorities
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Budget availability
- Employee performance and tenure
- Time commitment and impact on work

### Denial and Appeals
- If denied, employee receives explanation
- May resubmit with additional justification
- May appeal to HR Director
- Alternative lower-cost options may be suggested

## Commitment and Repayment

### Service Commitment Summary

| Development Type | Commitment Period | Repayment Threshold |
|------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Courses < $1,000 | None | N/A |
| Courses $1,000-$5,000 | 6 months | Pro-rated |
| Certifications | 1 year | Pro-rated |
| Tuition (see table in Tuition section) | 1-3 years | Pro-rated |

### Repayment Calculation
- Only voluntary resignation triggers repayment
- Prorated based on time remaining in commitment period
- Formula: (Total Reimbursement) × (Months Remaining / Total Commitment Months)
- Deducted from final paycheck where legally permitted
- Payment arrangement made if exceeds final pay

### Waiver of Repayment
Repayment waived for:
- Involuntary termination (layoff, elimination of position, downsizing)
- Termination due to disability
- Relocation beyond 50 miles at company request
- Special circumstances at discretion of HR Director

### Repayment Example
Employee received $12,000 tuition reimbursement (2-year commitment).
Employee resigns after 15 months.

Calculation:
- Commitment: 24 months
- Completed: 15 months
- Remaining: 9 months
- Repayment: $12,000 × (9/24) = $4,500

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use my budget for multiple small courses instead of one big conference?**
A: Yes, you can split your budget however makes sense for your learning goals.

**Q: What if I want to attend a conference that costs more than my annual budget?**
A: Discuss with your manager. Additional funding may be available for high-value opportunities.

**Q: Can I start a degree program before completing 1 year of employment?**
A: Tuition reimbursement requires 1 year of service. You may enroll but wouldn't be eligible for reimbursement until 1-year anniversary.

**Q: What happens to my commitment if I'm promoted or change roles internally?**
A: Commitment continues. Internal moves do not trigger repayment.

**Q: Can I attend a conference even if I'm not speaking?**
A: Yes, attending as participant is encouraged. Speaking engagements get priority for budget allocation.

**Q: Does my manager have to approve every Udemy course I take?**
A: For small purchases (<$100), manager may provide blanket approval. Larger expenses require per-request approval.

**Q: Can I get reimbursed for books from Amazon?**
A: Yes, professional/technical books related to your role are reimbursable. Submit receipt with expense report.
