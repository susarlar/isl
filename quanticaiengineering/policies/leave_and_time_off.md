## Policy Overview

### Purpose
This policy outlines time off benefits and leave programs available to Quantic AI Engineering employees. We recognize the importance of work-life balance and provide competitive time off benefits to support employee well-being.

### Scope
This policy applies to all regular full-time and part-time employees. Temporary and contract workers are not eligible unless specifically stated in their contracts. Benefits may vary for international employees based on local laws.

### Legal Compliance
This policy complies with:
- Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- State and local leave laws
- International labor laws (where applicable)

## Paid Time Off (PTO)

### PTO Philosophy
Quantic AI Engineering uses a combined PTO bank that can be used for vacation, personal days, appointments, or any other reason. We trust employees to manage their time responsibly.

### Accrual Rates

**Full-Time Employees:**

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Days | Accrual per Pay Period (bi-weekly) |
|-----------------|----------------|-----------------------------------|
| 0-2 years | 20 days (160 hours) | 6.15 hours |
| 3-5 years | 25 days (200 hours) | 7.69 hours |
| 6-10 years | 30 days (240 hours) | 9.23 hours |
| 11+ years | 35 days (280 hours) | 10.77 hours |

**Part-Time Employees:**
PTO accrues proportionally based on hours worked. For example, a part-time employee working 20 hours/week accrues 50% of the full-time rate.

### PTO Usage Guidelines

**Requesting PTO:**
- Submit requests through HRIS system (Workday) at least 2 weeks in advance
- Requests for 5+ consecutive days require 1 month advance notice
- Manager approval required before booking travel
- Last-minute requests considered on case-by-case basis for emergencies

**Approval Criteria:**
- Business needs and workload
- Adequate coverage available
- Other team members' scheduled time off
- Advance notice provided
- Critical project deadlines

**Blackout Periods:**
Certain departments may have blackout periods during busy seasons (e.g., end of fiscal year for Finance, product launch periods for Engineering). Blackout dates communicated at least 3 months in advance.

### Minimum Usage
Employees are encouraged to use at least 10 days of PTO annually for rest and rejuvenation. Managers should discuss time off during 1:1 meetings with employees who haven't taken PTO in 6+ months.

### Maximum Accrual
- Maximum accrual: 1.5x annual allotment
- Once maximum reached, accrual stops until PTO used
- Quarterly reminders sent to employees approaching maximum
- Forfeited accrual cannot be recovered

### PTO Payout
- Accrued, unused PTO paid out upon separation (where legally required)
- Payout amount capped at annual allotment (no excess carryover paid)
- Calculated at employee's base rate at time of separation
- Included in final paycheck

### PTO During Notice Period
- Employees may use accrued PTO during notice period with manager approval
- Company may require employee to work through notice period
- Unused PTO paid per state law

## Holidays

### Observed Holidays

Quantic AI Engineering observes the following paid holidays:

**Fixed Holidays (10 days):**
- New Year's Day - January 1
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day - Third Monday in January
- Presidents' Day - Third Monday in February
- Memorial Day - Last Monday in May
- Independence Day - July 4
- Labor Day - First Monday in September
- Thanksgiving Day - Fourth Thursday in November
- Day After Thanksgiving - Fourth Friday in November
- Christmas Eve - December 24
- Christmas Day - December 25

**Floating Holidays (3 days):**
Employees receive 3 floating holiday days annually to observe holidays meaningful to them (religious, cultural, personal).

- Must be requested at least 1 week in advance
- Expire December 31 (no carryover)
- Prorated for new hires based on start date

### Holiday Pay
- Full-time employees receive 8 hours of holiday pay per observed holiday
- Part-time employees (20+ hours/week) receive prorated holiday pay
- Part-time employees (<20 hours/week) are not eligible for holiday pay

### Holiday Falling on Weekend
- If holiday falls on Saturday, observed on Friday
- If holiday falls on Sunday, observed on Monday

### Holiday During PTO
If an observed holiday occurs during scheduled PTO, the holiday is not deducted from PTO balance.

### Work on Holiday
Non-exempt employees required to work on holidays receive:
- Regular pay for holiday (up to 8 hours)
- Time and one-half for hours actually worked
- Example: 8 hours holiday pay + 1.5x for 8 hours worked = 20 hours pay

Exempt employees may take alternate day off with manager approval.

## Sick Leave

### Sick Leave Accrual
Sick leave is provided as part of PTO bank. However, to comply with state paid sick leave laws, employees have access to:
- Minimum 5 days (40 hours) annually for illness
- Available immediately upon hire (no waiting period)
- Separate from PTO for tracking purposes only

### Eligible Uses
Sick leave may be used for:
- Employee's own illness, injury, or health condition
- Medical, dental, or vision appointments
- Preventative care
- Care for family member with illness or health condition
- Absences related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

**Family Member Defined:**
Child, parent, spouse/domestic partner, grandparent, grandchild, sibling

### Notification Requirements
- Notify supervisor as soon as possible, ideally before shift start
- For absences 3+ consecutive days, provide estimated return date
- Update manager on status if absence extends beyond initial estimate

### Medical Certification
Medical certification may be required for:
- Absences of 5+ consecutive days
- Suspected abuse of sick leave
- Intermittent absences in patterns (e.g., Mondays/Fridays)
- Accommodation requests related to medical condition

### Return to Work
- Medical clearance required for absences of 10+ consecutive days
- ADA interactive process for ongoing medical conditions
- Transitional work arrangements considered

### Sick Leave During PTO
Employees who become ill during scheduled vacation may request to convert PTO to sick leave with medical documentation.

## Family and Medical Leave

### FMLA Eligibility
Employees eligible for FMLA leave if they have:
- Worked for company for 12+ months (not necessarily consecutive)
- Worked 1,250+ hours in previous 12 months
- Work at location with 50+ employees within 75 miles

### Qualifying Reasons
FMLA leave available for:
- Birth and care of newborn child
- Adoption or foster care placement
- Care for spouse, child, or parent with serious health condition
- Employee's own serious health condition
- Qualifying exigency for military family member
- Care for covered military service member with serious injury/illness (up to 26 weeks)

### Leave Entitlement
- Up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave in 12-month rolling period
- Up to 26 weeks for military caregiver leave
- Leave may be taken continuously, intermittently, or as reduced schedule

### Pay During FMLA
- FMLA is unpaid, but employees must use accrued PTO concurrently
- After PTO exhausted, leave is unpaid
- State disability insurance may provide income replacement in some states

### Benefits During FMLA
- Company continues health insurance premium payments
- Employee responsible for their portion of premiums
- Failure to pay premiums may result in coverage loss
- Other benefits (PTO accrual, 401k match) suspended during unpaid leave

### Job Protection
- Employee restored to same or equivalent position upon return
- Seniority and benefits maintained
- No loss of accrued benefits

### Requesting FMLA Leave
1. Notify HR at least 30 days in advance (if foreseeable)
2. Complete FMLA application forms
3. Provide medical certification within 15 days
4. HR determines eligibility and provides notice of rights
5. HR coordinates leave and ensures compliance

### Medical Certification
- Required within 15 calendar days of request
- Must be completed by healthcare provider
- Company may request second opinion at its expense
- Recertification may be requested every 30 days for ongoing conditions

## Parental Leave

### Policy Statement
Quantic AI Engineering provides paid parental leave to support employees during this important life event.

### Eligibility
All employees who have completed 6 months of service are eligible.

### Leave Entitlement

**Birth Parent:**
- 16 weeks (640 hours) paid leave
- Must be taken within 12 months of child's birth
- First 6-8 weeks typically coincide with medical recovery (short-term disability)

**Non-Birth Parent:**
- 10 weeks (400 hours) paid leave
- Must be taken within 12 months of child's birth or placement

**Adoption/Foster Care:**
- 10 weeks (400 hours) paid leave
- Must be taken within 12 months of placement

### Pay During Parental Leave
- 100% base salary for eligible leave period
- Does not require use of accrued PTO
- Runs concurrently with FMLA
- Benefits continue as normal

### Requesting Parental Leave
1. Notify manager and HR as soon as possible (ideally 3+ months before expected leave)
2. Complete parental leave request form
3. HR coordinates leave and benefit continuation
4. Work with manager to create transition plan

### Flexible Return Options
- Phased return: Part-time schedule for first 4 weeks (80% pay)
- Delayed return: Extend leave using accrued PTO or unpaid leave
- Remote work: May request temporary remote work arrangement

### Bonding Leave Extension
After paid parental leave exhausted, employees may use:
- Accrued PTO
- Unpaid FMLA leave (if eligible and weeks remain)
- Unpaid personal leave

## Bereavement Leave

### Policy
Paid bereavement leave provided to allow employees to grieve and attend funeral services.

### Immediate Family
**5 days (40 hours) paid leave for death of:**
- Spouse/domestic partner
- Child (including stepchild, foster child)
- Parent (including stepparent, parent-in-law)
- Sibling (including stepsibling)

### Extended Family
**3 days (24 hours) paid leave for death of:**
- Grandparent
- Grandchild
- Aunt/uncle
- Niece/nephew

### Other
**1 day (8 hours) paid leave for death of:**
- Close friend
- Cousin
- Other relative

### Usage
- Must be used within 30 days of death
- Need not be consecutive days
- Additional time off available using PTO or unpaid leave
- Manager discretion for extended bereavement needs

### Documentation
Death certificate or funeral program may be requested for leaves of 3+ days.

### Travel
If extensive travel required for services, manager may approve additional PTO or unpaid leave.

## Jury Duty and Voting

### Jury Duty
**Policy:**
Employees summoned for jury duty will be granted time off with pay.

**Pay:**
- Full pay for up to 10 days of jury service
- After 10 days, unpaid leave (employee may use PTO)
- Jury duty fees retained by employee

**Notification:**
- Provide copy of jury summons to manager and HR as soon as received
- Notify manager of schedule as soon as known

**Work Requirements:**
- If excused from jury duty for 4+ hours, employee expected to report to work
- Report to work on days not required for jury service
- Keep manager informed of schedule changes

### Voting
**Time Off to Vote:**
- Polls open outside working hours: No time off needed
- Polls not open outside working hours: Up to 2 hours paid time off
- Coordinate with manager for least disruptive time
- Advanced notice appreciated but not required

**Civic Engagement:**
- Encouraged to participate in civic process
- No discrimination based on voting choices
- Political discussions at work should remain respectful

## Leave Administration

### Requesting Leave
- Submit requests through Workday HRIS system
- Include dates, type of leave, and reason (if required)
- Manager notification in addition to system request
- Emergency requests via phone/email followed by system submission

### Approval Process
- Manager reviews and approves/denies within 3 business days
- HR approves statutory leaves (FMLA, military, etc.)
- Denials include reason and discussion with employee

### Tracking and Recordkeeping
- All leave tracked in Workday
- Employees can view balances in self-service portal
- Accruals, usage, and balances on pay stubs
- HR maintains leave documentation per legal requirements

### Intermittent Leave
- Available for FMLA-qualifying conditions
- Medical certification required
- Reasonable advance notice when possible
- May need to account for time in 15-minute increments

### Unpaid Leave
When all paid leave exhausted:
- Benefits continue for up to 30 days of unpaid leave
- After 30 days, COBRA offered for health insurance
- Other benefits (PTO accrual, 401k match) suspended
- Job protection depends on type of leave (FMLA vs. personal)

### Return from Leave
- Notify manager and HR of return date at least 1 week in advance
- Medical clearance for returns from medical leave
- Return to same or equivalent position
- Schedule return meeting with manager

### Leave Coordination
Multiple types of leave may run concurrently:
- FMLA + Short-term disability + Parental leave
- State paid family leave + Company benefits
- HR coordinates to ensure maximum benefit

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I cash out unused PTO?**
A: PTO cannot be cashed out during employment, only upon separation (where required by law).

**Q: What happens to my PTO if I'm terminated?**
A: Accrued, unused PTO paid per state law. Some states require payout, others do not.

**Q: Can I donate PTO to a coworker?**
A: We do not currently have a PTO donation program, but this may be considered in special circumstances.

**Q: Do I get paid for holidays during unpaid leave?**
A: No, you must be in active employment status immediately before and after the holiday.

**Q: Can I take FMLA leave for my pet's surgery?**
A: No, FMLA only covers care for family members as defined by law.
