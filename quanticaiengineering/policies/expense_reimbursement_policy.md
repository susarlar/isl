## Policy Overview

### Purpose
This policy establishes guidelines for business-related expenses incurred by employees on behalf of Quantic AI Engineering. The policy ensures appropriate use of company funds, compliance with tax regulations, and timely reimbursement.

### Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors who incur expenses on behalf of the company.

### General Principles
- Expenses must be reasonable, necessary, and directly related to business
- Choose most economical option appropriate for circumstances
- Obtain manager pre-approval for expenses over $500
- Submit expense reports within 30 days of incurrence
- Provide itemized receipts for all expenses over $25

## Travel Expenses

### Air Travel

**Booking Requirements:**
- Book through approved travel platform (Concur Travel)
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible
- Choose most economical option (typically economy class)
- Avoid first-class and business-class (unless flight >6 hours, then business-class permitted)

**Change Fees:**
- Reimbursable if change due to business reasons
- Employee responsible for personal change fees
- Travel insurance recommended for non-refundable tickets

**Baggage Fees:**
- One checked bag reimbursable
- Additional bags require business justification
- Overweight fees generally not reimbursable

**Frequent Flyer Miles:**
- Employee may keep miles/points earned
- Company will not reimburse for tickets purchased with personal miles

### Ground Transportation

**Personal Vehicle Mileage:**
- Reimbursed at IRS standard mileage rate ($0.67/mile for 2026)
- Mileage calculated from office or home (whichever is shorter)
- Submit mileage log with starting/ending locations and business purpose
- Tolls and parking separately reimbursable

**Rental Cars:**
- Economy or mid-size vehicles appropriate for most situations
- Full-size or SUV only when business need requires (e.g., multiple passengers, equipment transport)
- Decline collision damage waiver (company insurance provides coverage)
- Refuel before returning to avoid premium charges
- Pre-pay for fuel only if significantly cheaper

**Rideshare/Taxi:**
- Appropriate for airport transfers and local client meetings
- Provide itemized receipt showing pickup/drop-off locations
- Standard service level (UberX, Lyft); premium services require justification
- Tip up to 20% reimbursable

**Public Transportation:**
- Appropriate for travel in cities with good transit systems
- More economical than taxis/rideshare
- Keep receipts (e.g., metro cards, train tickets)

### Lodging

**Hotel Selection:**
- Book through Concur Travel or approved booking sites
- Choose hotels offering government or corporate rates
- Standard room sufficient; suites require business justification
- Consider location to minimize transportation costs

**Rate Guidelines:**
- Maximum $250/night for primary metro areas (NYC, SF, LA, DC, Chicago, Boston)
- Maximum $175/night for secondary cities
- Maximum $150/night for other locations
- Exceptions require manager pre-approval

**Additional Charges:**
- WiFi reimbursable if not included
- Resort fees reimbursable (unavoidable)
- In-room movies, minibar, spa services: NOT reimbursable
- Parking reimbursable if required

### Travel Duration
- Book return travel for same day when feasible
- Overnight stays justified when meeting spans multiple days or early morning start time
- Personal time added to business trip: Only incremental costs reimbursed

## Meals and Entertainment

### Meal Allowances

**While Traveling:**
- Breakfast: $20
- Lunch: $25
- Dinner: $50
- Amounts are per-person maximums, not daily stipends

**Local Meals (not traveling):**
- Generally not reimbursable
- Exception: Working meal (e.g., lunch meeting with colleague on project) up to $25/person

### Business Meals with Clients

**Guidelines:**
- Business purpose required (identify attendees and topics discussed)
- Choose appropriate venue (not overly extravagant)
- Alcohol permitted in moderation (max 2 drinks per person)
- Tip 15-20% (before tax)

**Limits:**
- $100 per person for standard client meal
- $150 per person for special occasions (large deal closing, executive visit)
- Higher amounts require VP approval in advance

### Itemized Receipts Required
- Credit card receipt alone insufficient
- Must provide itemized receipt showing:
  - Restaurant name and location
  - Date and time
  - Items ordered
  - Subtotal, tax, tip
  - Payment method
- Missing receipts: Limit $25; supervisor attestation required

### Alcohol
- Permitted at business meals with clients
- Moderate consumption (1-2 drinks)
- Never excessive drinking
- Follow client's lead; don't order if client doesn't
- Not reimbursable for solo meals while traveling

### Entertainment Events

**Client Entertainment:**
- Sporting events, concerts, theater tickets reimbursable when entertaining clients
- Must attend with client (cannot give tickets to client)
- Document business purpose and attendees
- Reasonable cost based on event and client relationship
- Pre-approval required for events >$500

**Team Events:**
- Department budgets for team building
- Not submitted as individual expense reports
- Coordinate with department manager

## Submission and Approval

### Expense Report Requirements

**Submission Deadline:**
- Submit within 30 days of expense incurrence
- Late submissions may not be reimbursed
- Month-end travelers: Submit by 5th of following month

**Required Information:**
- Business purpose for each expense
- Project code or client matter (if applicable)
- Attendees for meals and entertainment
- Itemized receipts for expenses >$25
- Mileage logs for vehicle use

**Submission Process:**
1. Log into Concur Expense
2. Create new expense report
3. Upload receipts (photo or PDF)
4. Enter expense details
5. Assign to appropriate GL account
6. Submit for approval

### Approval Workflow
- Direct manager approves expense reports
- Finance reviews for policy compliance
- CFO approval required for reports >$5,000
- Non-compliant items kicked back for correction or removal

### Reimbursement Timing
- Approved expenses reimbursed via direct deposit
- Standard processing: 5-7 business days from approval
- Expedited processing available for hardship situations (contact Finance)

### Currency Conversion
- International expenses converted to USD at date of transaction
- Use credit card conversion rate or daily exchange rate
- Document exchange rate used

### Tax Implications
- Most business expense reimbursements are non-taxable
- Personal expenses or non-compliant reimbursements may be taxable
- Finance determines tax treatment

## Corporate Credit Cards

### Eligibility
Corporate credit cards issued to:
- Employees who travel regularly (5+ trips/year)
- Senior managers and executives
- Other positions at manager discretion

### Application Process
- Request through Finance department
- Credit check may be required
- Employee signs cardholder agreement

### Usage Guidelines
- Use ONLY for business expenses
- Never for personal expenses (even if planning to reimburse company)
- Report lost or stolen cards immediately
- Retain receipts for all charges

### Statement Reconciliation
- Review charges in Concur monthly
- Assign expense type and business purpose to each charge
- Upload supporting receipts
- Submit for approval by statement due date
- Unreconciled charges may result in card suspension

### Personal Charges
- Strictly prohibited
- If personal charge made accidentally, reimburse company immediately
- Repeated personal charges result in card revocation and possible disciplinary action

### Card Limits
- Standard employee card: $5,000 monthly limit
- Manager card: $10,000 monthly limit
- Executive card: $25,000 monthly limit
- Temporary limit increases available for specific business needs

### Termination
- Employee must return card on last day of employment
- Final statement reconciled before final paycheck
- Unreimbursed charges deducted from final pay (where legally permitted)

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are NOT reimbursable:

### Personal Expenses
- Personal meals (when not traveling)
- Commuting costs to/from regular office
- Gym memberships or fitness classes
- Personal grooming (haircuts, dry cleaning, etc.)
- Personal entertainment or recreation
- Traffic tickets, parking fines, towing fees

### Travel-Related
- Travel insurance (domestic travel)
- Companion/spouse travel costs (unless approved in advance for specific business purpose)
- Pet boarding or pet travel fees
- Personal side trips or vacation days added to business travel
- Luggage purchases (unless damaged by airline)

### Excessive or Unreasonable
- First-class airfare (unless flight >6 hours)
- Luxury vehicle rentals without justification
- Hotel suites without business need
- Excessive meals or entertainment
- Purchase of major equipment without pre-approval

### Entertainment
- In-room movies or adult entertainment
- Minibar charges
- Spa services or hotel fitness center fees
- Casino gambling

### Other
- Childcare or pet care
- Personal phone calls (international calls may be reimbursable)
- Lost personal items
- Costs reimbursed by client or third party

## Special Situations

### Relocation Expenses
Covered under separate Relocation Policy. Contact HR for details.

### Interviewing Candidates
- Candidate travel expenses reimbursed by company
- Do not pay candidate directly; have candidate submit expenses to HR
- Reasonable meal expenses when hosting candidate

### Conferences and Trade Shows
- Registration fees pre-approved
- Follow standard travel and meal guidelines
- Booth staff expenses: Coordinate with Marketing
- Promotional items/giveaways: Approved in advance through Marketing budget

### International Travel
- Passport and visa fees reimbursable for business travel
- International cell phone plan or SIM card reimbursable
- Travel vaccinations reimbursable
- Travel insurance recommended and reimbursable
- Per diem rates follow GSA guidelines for international locations

### Extended Travel (30+ consecutive days)
- May negotiate corporate housing rates
- Laundry service reimbursable (reasonable amounts)
- Weekend return trips home may be reimbursable (case-by-case)
- Contact Finance for guidance on extended assignments

## Policy Violations

### Consequences
Violations may result in:
- Non-reimbursement of expenses
- Revocation of corporate credit card
- Disciplinary action
- Termination for serious or repeated violations
- Legal action for fraudulent submissions

### Examples of Violations
- Submitting personal expenses
- Submitting false or inflated expenses
- Submitting duplicate expense claims
- Forging receipts or documentation
- Splitting expenses to avoid approval thresholds
- Claiming mileage for trips not taken

### Reporting Violations
Suspected violations should be reported to:
- Finance Department: finance@quanticai.com
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS (anonymous)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I submit expenses without receipts?**
A: Receipts required for expenses >$25. For smaller amounts, supervisor attestation may suffice.

**Q: What if I lost my receipt?**
A: Provide credit card statement and written explanation. Manager must approve. Limited to $25/item and $100/report.

**Q: Can I buy a gift for a client?**
A: Yes, reasonable gifts ($100 or less) for appropriate occasions. Document business purpose.

**Q: Am I required to book through Concur?**
A: For airfare and hotels, yes. Other travel arrangements may be booked directly but should still seek best rates.

**Q: Can I use my personal credit card?**
A: Yes, then submit for reimbursement. Corporate card preferred for frequent travelers.

**Q: What if my expense report is denied?**
A: You'll receive explanation. You may provide additional documentation or appeal to Finance.

**Q: Can I extend a business trip for personal vacation?**
A: Yes, but only incremental costs reimbursed. If personal stay makes trip more expensive, company reimburses only what business-only trip would have cost.

**Q: Are tips included in meal limits?**
A: Yes, tip is included in the maximum amount. Tip 15-20% and stay within total limit.
