## Policy Overview

### Purpose
This policy provides guidelines for social media use by Quantic AI Engineering employees, both personally and professionally. Social media is a powerful communication tool, and we want to ensure employees understand expectations and protect both personal and company interests.

### Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, contractors)
- All social media platforms (Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, Reddit, YouTube, blogs, forums, etc.)
- Posts made on personal and professional accounts
- Posts made during and outside work hours
- Posts identifying connection to Quantic AI Engineering

### Definitions

**Social Media:** Web-based and mobile platforms for creating, sharing, and exchanging content and engaging in social networking. Includes:
- Social networks (Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter/X)
- Video sharing (YouTube, TikTok, Vimeo)
- Photo sharing (Instagram, Snapchat, Pinterest)
- Blogs and microblogs
- Forums and discussion boards
- Review sites (Glassdoor, Indeed)
- Wikis
- Any similar platforms

**Personal Social Media:** Accounts created and maintained by individuals for personal use.

**Professional Social Media:** Accounts identifying employee's professional role or connection to company (e.g., LinkedIn profile mentioning employment).

**Official Company Social Media:** Accounts owned, managed, and operated by Quantic AI Engineering.

### Guiding Principles
- **Transparency:** Be honest and authentic
- **Respect:** Treat others with respect and professionalism
- **Responsibility:** Think before you post; you're accountable for your content
- **Privacy:** Protect confidential company and customer information
- **Compliance:** Follow all laws and regulations

## Personal Social Media Use

### Employee Rights
Quantic AI Engineering respects employees' rights to:
- Maintain personal social media accounts
- Express personal opinions on matters of public concern
- Discuss wages, hours, and working conditions (protected concerted activity under NLRA)
- Use social media during personal time
- Privacy in personal affairs

### Personal Use During Work Hours
- Minimal personal social media use during work hours is acceptable
- Should not interfere with job responsibilities or productivity
- Keep use to breaks and lunch periods when possible
- Excessive use may result in disciplinary action
- Follow company's Acceptable Use Policy for internet access

### Identifying Connection to Company
If you identify yourself as a Quantic AI Engineering employee on personal social media:
- Include disclaimer: "Views expressed are my own and do not represent my employer"
- Recognize that your posts may be associated with the company
- Exercise good judgment in content you post
- Maintain professional image befitting a company representative

**Recommended Disclaimer:**
"Opinions shared here are my own and do not reflect the views of my employer, Quantic AI Engineering."

### Personal Brand vs. Company Brand
- Your personal social media is YOUR space
- You may share company content (blog posts, job openings, press releases) if desired
- Not required to follow, like, or share company content
- Not required to identify employer on personal accounts
- If you do identify company connection, follow this policy's guidelines

## Professional Social Media Use

### LinkedIn and Professional Networking
Employees are encouraged to:
- Maintain updated LinkedIn profiles
- List Quantic AI Engineering as current employer (if comfortable doing so)
- Include accurate job title and description
- Connect with colleagues and industry professionals
- Share industry insights and company news
- Recommend and endorse colleagues

**Profile Guidelines:**
- Use professional profile photo
- Accurate work history and credentials
- Proofread profile content for errors
- Avoid controversial or offensive content
- Update when changing roles

### Sharing Company Content
You may share:
- Published blog posts from company blog
- Press releases and public announcements
- Job openings
- Product launches (after public release)
- Company event photos (with permission of individuals shown)
- Awards and recognition

Do NOT share:
- Confidential information
- Unreleased products or features
- Financial information not publicly disclosed
- Customer information or case studies without approval
- Internal communications or documents
- Proprietary processes or trade secrets

### Speaking on Industry Topics
Employees with expertise in relevant fields are encouraged to:
- Share knowledge and insights on industry topics
- Establish thought leadership
- Participate in professional online communities
- Write blog posts or articles
- Speak at conferences and events

When speaking on industry topics:
- Make clear whether speaking in personal or professional capacity
- Don't claim to represent company unless authorized
- Don't disclose confidential company information
- Focus on your expertise and general industry knowledge
- Include disclaimer if discussing matters that could be seen as company-related

### Employee Advocacy Program
Quantic AI Engineering has an optional employee advocacy program:
- Employees can opt in to receive curated content to share
- Content pre-approved by Marketing
- Participants receive recognition and rewards
- Not mandatory; participation voluntary
- Contact marketing@quanticai.com to join

## Guidelines and Best Practices

### Think Before You Post
Before posting content related to work, ask yourself:
- Would I be comfortable saying this in a company meeting?
- Could this harm the company's reputation or relationships?
- Am I disclosing confidential information?
- Am I violating someone's privacy?
- Could this be misunderstood or taken out of context?
- Does this align with company values?

If answer to any is "no" or "maybe," don't post.

### Confidentiality
Never disclose:
- Unreleased products, features, or strategies
- Financial information (revenue, projections, deals)
- Customer data or information
- Employee data (salaries, headcount, org changes)
- Security vulnerabilities or incidents
- Legal matters or disputes
- Merger and acquisition discussions
- Proprietary technology or algorithms
- Internal processes or methodologies
- Board or executive discussions

When in doubt, DON'T share. Ask Marketing or Legal if unclear.

### Respect and Professionalism
- Be respectful of others (colleagues, customers, competitors, public figures)
- No harassment, bullying, or discriminatory language
- No profanity or offensive content
- Respectful disagreement is okay; personal attacks are not
- Remember: Internet is permanent; screenshots exist forever

### Accuracy and Honesty
- Ensure information is accurate before sharing
- Correct errors promptly if made
- Don't exaggerate or make false claims
- Cite sources when sharing others' content
- Be transparent about your relationship to company

### Privacy
Respect others' privacy:
- Don't post photos of colleagues without permission
- Don't share personal information about others
- Don't disclose who works at company without their consent
- Don't "check in" at company locations without considering security
- Be cautious about revealing workplace details that could pose security risks

### Copyright and Intellectual Property
- Respect copyright and trademark laws
- Don't use others' content without permission or proper attribution
- Use only licensed images or create original content
- Don't infringe on competitors' intellectual property
- Company content is owned by company; don't claim as your own

### Responding to Negative Comments
If you encounter negative comments about the company:
- Stay calm and don't react emotionally
- Don't engage in arguments
- Alert appropriate team (Marketing, Customer Success, HR, Legal depending on nature)
- Let authorized spokespersons handle official responses
- If commenting personally, be constructive and empathetic; acknowledge concerns without making commitments

### Political and Social Issues
- You have the right to express personal political and social views on personal accounts
- Make clear these are personal views, not company's
- Avoid implying company endorses your views
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints
- Consider that divisive political posts may affect professional relationships
- Company's official positions on social issues communicated by Executive Leadership only

### Crisis Situations
During company crisis (security breach, major outage, negative news):
- Refer questions to official company statements
- Don't speculate or provide unofficial information
- Don't comment on situation without authorization
- Coordinate with Communications team
- Personal support for company is appreciated but should be factual and appropriate

## Prohibited Activities

The following activities on social media are prohibited and may result in discipline:

### Confidentiality Violations
- Disclosing proprietary or confidential information
- Sharing unreleased product information
- Posting confidential documents or screenshots
- Discussing financial information not publicly available

### Harassment and Discrimination
- Harassing, bullying, or intimidating others
- Discriminatory or hateful comments based on protected characteristics
- Sexual harassment or inappropriate sexual content
- Creating hostile work environment through online behavior

### Misrepresentation
- Claiming to speak for company without authorization
- Impersonating company officials or creating fake accounts
- Making false or misleading statements about company
- Misrepresenting qualifications or role

### Intellectual Property Infringement
- Using copyrighted materials without permission
- Infringing trademarks
- Sharing proprietary code or algorithms

### Competitive Intelligence
- Posing as someone else to gather competitive intelligence
- Accessing competitors' systems or proprietary information
- Disparaging competitors with false information

### Legal Violations
- Defamation or libel
- Securities violations (insider trading, selective disclosure)
- Violations of regulatory requirements
- Encouraging illegal activities

### Reputational Harm
- Conduct that damages company's reputation
- Associating company with illegal, unethical, or offensive activities
- Posting content while identifying as company employee that reflects poorly on company

### Company Resource Misuse
- Excessive personal social media use during work hours
- Using company equipment for inappropriate social media activities
- Violating company's Acceptable Use or Information Security policies

## Consequences of Violations

### Progressive Discipline
Violations may result in:
1. Verbal warning and coaching
2. Written warning
3. Suspension
4. Termination

Severity of consequences depends on:
- Nature and severity of violation
- Impact on company, customers, or colleagues
- Whether violation was intentional or negligent
- Employee's disciplinary history

### Serious Violations
Immediate termination may result from:
- Intentional disclosure of confidential information
- Harassment or discrimination
- Misrepresentation as company spokesperson
- Actions causing significant reputational or financial harm
- Legal violations

### Legal Consequences
Certain violations may result in:
- Civil lawsuits (defamation, harassment, breach of confidentiality)
- Criminal charges (hacking, threats, insider trading)
- Regulatory penalties
- Loss of professional licenses or certifications

### Remediation
In some cases, employee may be required to:
- Remove offending content
- Post correction or apology
- Complete additional training
- Agree to monitoring of social media activity
