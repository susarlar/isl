## Policy Overview

### Purpose
This policy establishes guidelines for professional communication using company-provided communication tools. Proper use of these systems ensures effective business communication, protects sensitive information, and maintains professional standards.

### Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and temporary workers
- All company-provided communication systems
- Communication during and outside business hours
- Communication using company equipment and accounts

### Covered Systems
- Email (Microsoft Outlook)
- Instant messaging (Microsoft Teams)
- Video conferencing (Teams, Zoom)
- Phone systems (desk phones, company mobile phones)
- Collaboration tools (SharePoint, OneDrive)
- Other approved communication platforms

### Monitoring and Privacy
**Important Notice:** Company communication systems are provided for business purposes. The company reserves the right to:
- Monitor, access, and review all communications
- Retrieve deleted messages and files
- Analyze communication patterns and content
- Disclose communications when required by law or business need

**Limited Privacy Expectation:**
- Communications using company systems are not private
- Personal use should be minimal and non-sensitive
- Sensitive personal matters should use personal devices/accounts
- Company may review communications during investigations, litigation, or regulatory requests

## Email Guidelines

### Email System
- Platform: Microsoft Outlook / Office 365
- Format: firstname.lastname@quanticai.com
- Mobile access available via Outlook app
- Web access: outlook.office.com

### Professional Email Standards

**Subject Lines:**
- Clear and descriptive
- Summarize email purpose
- Use action-oriented language when appropriate (e.g., "ACTION REQUIRED: Approve Q1 Budget")
- Avoid vague subjects ("Quick question," "Hi")

**Email Body:**
- Professional greeting appropriate to relationship
- Clear and concise message
- Proper grammar and spelling
- Logical structure (use paragraphs, bullet points)
- Professional closing with signature

**Tone:**
- Professional and respectful
- Avoid ALL CAPS (appears as shouting)
- Be mindful of tone in written communication
- When emotional, draft email and review before sending
- Consider phone call for sensitive or complex topics

**Signature:**
Include:
- Full name
- Job title
- Company name
- Phone number
- Email address
- Optional: LinkedIn profile, pronouns

### Response Time Expectations
- Respond to internal emails within 24 hours (during business days)
- Respond to customer emails within 4 hours during business hours
- Use auto-reply/out-of-office when unavailable
- Acknowledge receipt if full response will be delayed

### To, CC, and BCC

**To:** Direct recipients requiring action or response
**CC (Carbon Copy):** Individuals who should be informed but no action required
**BCC (Blind Carbon Copy):** Use sparingly; appropriate for:
- Large distribution lists (protects recipient privacy)
- Sensitive situations where recipient list should not be shared
- NOT for secret surveillance of communications

**CC Guidelines:**
- Don't "CC everyone" - be selective
- Don't Reply All unless all need to see response
- Remove unnecessary recipients when responding
- Respect others' time and inbox management

### Attachments
- Keep under 25 MB (use OneDrive/SharePoint links for larger files)
- Use descriptive file names (not "Document1.docx")
- Scan attachments for viruses
- Mention attachments in email body
- Double-check you attached correct file before sending

### Email Security

**Never Send via Email:**
- Social Security numbers
- Credit card numbers (full)
- Passwords or authentication credentials
- Highly confidential documents (use secure sharing methods)
- Sensitive customer data

**Encrypted Email:**
Use encryption for:
- Confidential business information
- Personal identifying information (PII)
- Financial data
- Legal matters
- Requested by recipient

**How to Encrypt:** Add [Secure] or [Encrypt] to subject line (Office 365 auto-encrypts)

### Phishing and Suspicious Emails

**Red Flags:**
- Unexpected attachments or links
- Requests for personal or financial information
- Urgent demands for action
- Grammatical errors or odd formatting
- Sender email doesn't match supposed organization
- Too good to be true offers

**If You Receive Suspicious Email:**
1. Do NOT click links or open attachments
2. Do NOT reply or provide information
3. Report to IT Security (forward to phishing@quanticai.com)
4. Delete from inbox after reporting

**If You Clicked a Suspicious Link:**
1. Disconnect from network (unplug Ethernet or disable Wi-Fi)
2. Contact IT Security immediately (Ext. 5200)
3. Change password from different device
4. Report incident per Security Policy

### Email Retention
- Most emails automatically retained for 3 years
- Deleted emails recoverable for 30 days
- Legal holds may extend retention
- Don't delete emails related to litigation or investigation
- Archive old emails to free up mailbox space (IT can assist)

### Out of Office / Auto-Reply
When out of office:
- Set auto-reply with return date
- Provide alternate contact for urgent matters
- Update calendar availability
- Clear inbox or delegate monitoring to colleague

**Auto-Reply Template:**
"Thank you for your email. I am out of the office [dates] with limited access to email. I will respond to your message upon my return on [date]. For urgent matters, please contact [Name] at [email] or [phone]."

### Email Distribution Lists
- IT manages company-wide distribution lists
- Request changes via IT ticket
- Use appropriately (don't spam large lists)
- Consider whether announcement necessary for all recipients

## Instant Messaging and Chat

### Platform
Primary tool: Microsoft Teams
- Real-time messaging
- File sharing
- Audio/video calls
- Channel-based collaboration

### When to Use Chat vs. Email
**Use Chat For:**
- Quick questions
- Time-sensitive matters
- Informal coordination
- Real-time collaboration
- Checking availability

**Use Email For:**
- Formal communications
- External recipients
- Detailed information
- Official requests and approvals
- Documentation and record-keeping
- When recipient may not be immediately available

### Chat Etiquette

**Status Indicators:**
- Set status accurately (Available, Busy, Do Not Disturb, Away)
- Respect others' status (don't disturb if DND unless emergency)
- Use "Busy" during meetings or focused work

**Message Formatting:**
- Keep messages concise
- Break long messages into multiple shorter ones
- Use @mentions to get specific person's attention
- Use channels for team discussions
- Use direct messages for 1:1 conversations

**Response Time:**
- Respond to chats within 30 minutes during work hours
- Not expected to respond after hours unless on-call
- "Thumbs up" or quick acknowledgment acceptable

**Professional Standards:**
- Maintain professionalism (even in casual chats)
- No inappropriate jokes or content
- Use emojis sparingly and appropriately
- Avoid chatting while in meetings (unless discussing meeting content)

**Group Chats and Channels:**
- Stay on topic
- Use threads to keep conversations organized
- Mute channels if notifications overwhelming
- Don't spam channels with off-topic content
- Use appropriate channels (don't post in wrong channel)

### File Sharing in Teams
- Share files directly in chat or channel
- Files automatically saved to SharePoint
- Use co-authoring for real-time collaboration
- Version control automatic

### Video and Audio Calls
- Use Teams for quick calls
- Schedule longer calls as meetings with agenda
- See Video Conferencing section for additional guidelines

## Video Conferencing

### Platforms
- **Internal meetings:** Microsoft Teams
- **External meetings:** Teams or Zoom (with client preference)
- **Large events/webinars:** Zoom Webinar or Teams Live Event

### Before the Meeting

**Technical Preparation:**
- Test audio and video beforehand
- Ensure adequate lighting
- Use headset or headphones for better audio quality
- Charge laptop or ensure plugged in
- Close unnecessary applications

**Background:**
- Use professional background or blur background
- Ensure background is clean and appropriate
- No inappropriate content visible
- Adequate lighting on your face

### During the Meeting

**Video:**
- Keep camera on when possible (creates engagement)
- Camera optional for large meetings or when bandwidth limited
- Look at camera (not screen) when speaking
- Minimize movement and distractions

**Audio:**
- Mute when not speaking (especially large meetings)
- Unmute to speak
- Announce yourself if joining by phone
- Avoid background noise (typing, rustling papers)

**Participation:**
- Join on time
- Introduce yourself if others may not know you
- Pay attention (don't multitask visibly)
- Use "raise hand" feature to request to speak
- Use chat for questions without interrupting
- Stay until meeting ends (or excuse yourself if must leave early)

**Screen Sharing:**
- Share specific application or window (not entire screen)
- Close confidential or personal content before sharing
- Ask before recording meeting
- Notify participants when recording

### Meeting Etiquette
- Be present and engaged
- No eating (drinking coffee/water acceptable)
- Dress appropriately
- Professional language and behavior
- Respect speaking turns
- Avoid interrupting

### Recording Meetings
- Obtain consent before recording
- Announce at beginning that meeting is recorded
- Inform participants of recording purpose and retention
- Store recordings securely
- Delete when no longer needed
- Comply with privacy laws (some states require all-party consent)

## Phone and Voicemail

### Company Phone System
- Desk phones provided to on-site employees
- Mobile phones provided to eligible positions
- Softphone available for remote workers
- International calling available (manager approval may be required)

### Answering Phone
**Professional Greeting:**
"Good [morning/afternoon], this is [Name]. How may I help you?"

Or: "[Company name], [Your name] speaking."

- Answer promptly (within 3 rings)
- Speak clearly and professionally
- Take notes during call
- Follow up as promised

### Voicemail
**Set Up Voicemail:**
- Record professional greeting with your name
- Indicate when you'll return calls
- Provide alternative contact if urgent

**Greeting Template:**
"Hello, you've reached [Name] at Quantic AI Engineering. I'm unable to take your call right now. Please leave your name, number, and a brief message, and I'll return your call within 24 hours. For urgent matters, please contact [Name/Department] at [number]. Thank you."

**Checking Voicemail:**
- Check at least twice daily
- Return calls within 24 hours
- Update greeting when out of office

### Mobile Phone Usage
If company provides mobile phone:
- Use primarily for business
- Reasonable personal use acceptable
- Protect device per Security Policy
- Report lost/stolen immediately
- Return upon separation

### Personal Cell Phone Use at Work
- Keep personal calls brief and private
- Take personal calls away from workspace
- Silence phone during meetings
- Don't use phone while walking (safety hazard)
- Excessive personal use may be addressed by manager

## Document Sharing and Collaboration

### Approved Platforms
- **SharePoint:** Document libraries, team sites
- **OneDrive for Business:** Personal work files
- **Microsoft Teams:** Team collaboration, file sharing
- **Approved secure file transfer:** For external sharing

### SharePoint Best Practices
- Organize with clear folder structure
- Use descriptive file names and metadata
- Delete outdated documents
- Set appropriate permissions
- Use version history and check-in/check-out

### OneDrive for Business
- Store work files only (not personal files)
- Sync to desktop for offline access
- Share links instead of email attachments
- Set expiration dates for shared links
- Review shared files periodically

### Co-Authoring
- Multiple users can edit simultaneously
- Changes saved automatically
- See others' cursors in real-time
- Use comments for feedback
- Resolve conflicts promptly

### External File Sharing
**Approved Methods:**
- SharePoint external sharing (with expiration date)
- Secure file transfer platform (IT provided)
- Encrypted email attachments

**NOT Approved:**
- Personal cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive personal)
- Unsecured public file sharing sites
- USB drives for confidential information

### Document Classification
Label documents with appropriate classification:
- **Public:** Marketing materials, public information
- **Internal:** Business-as-usual documents
- **Confidential:** Sensitive business information
- **Restricted:** Highly sensitive, regulated data

Follow Data Privacy Policy for handling each classification.

## Communication Security

### Password and Authentication
- Use strong passwords (see Information Security Policy)
- Enable multi-factor authentication (required)
- Don't share passwords or credentials
- Lock computer when away from desk
- Log out when finished on shared devices

### Email Security Best Practices
- Verify sender before clicking links or opening attachments
- Hover over links to see actual destination
- Be suspicious of unexpected emails with attachments
- Don't send sensitive information via unencrypted email
- Use encrypted email for confidential information

### Mobile Device Security
- Use device PIN/password
- Enable remote wipe capability
- Don't access company email on public computers
- Use VPN on public Wi-Fi
- Report lost/stolen devices immediately

### Phishing Prevention
- Complete annual security awareness training
- Report suspicious emails to phishing@quanticai.com
- Never provide credentials in response to email
- Verify requests for sensitive information through alternate channel (phone call)

### Video Conferencing Security
- Don't share meeting links publicly
- Use waiting room for external meetings
- Lock meeting once all participants joined
- Use passwords for sensitive meetings
- Remove disruptive participants if necessary

## Acceptable Use

### Appropriate Business Use
Communication systems should be used for:
- Business communications
- Professional development
- Collaboration with colleagues
- Communication with customers, partners, vendors
- Authorized company business

### Personal Use
Limited personal use acceptable if:
- Doesn't interfere with work responsibilities
- Complies with all policies
- Not excessive in duration or frequency
- Doesn't consume significant bandwidth or storage
- Not for personal business or profit

### Prohibited Uses
The following are prohibited:
- Harassment, discrimination, or hostile communication
- Offensive, obscene, or inappropriate content
- Illegal activities
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Personal commercial purposes or profit
- Chain letters, spam, or junk mail
- Excessive personal use
- Impersonating others
- Accessing others' accounts without authorization
- Introducing malware or viruses
- Circumventing security measures
- Violating copyright or intellectual property rights

### Consequences
Violations may result in:
- Warning and counseling
- Suspension of access privileges
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Legal action
- Criminal prosecution (if illegal activity)

## Special Situations

### Communication During Investigations
If communications subject to investigation or litigation:
- Legal hold may be placed on accounts
- Do not delete any communications
- Cooperate with IT and Legal
- Maintain confidentiality of investigation

### Departing Employees
Upon resignation or termination:
- Email access revoked on last day
- Forwarding may be set up temporarily
- IT archives mailbox per retention policy
- Return all company devices
- Personal emails: Request export before last day (IT reviews for business content)

### Extended Leave
For extended absence (medical leave, parental leave, etc.):
- Set out-of-office message
- Delegate monitoring to colleague if appropriate
- Disable login if security concern
- Account maintained during approved leave

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I access company email from my personal device?**
A: Yes, using Outlook mobile app with MDM enrollment. Contact IT for setup.

**Q: Can I use personal email for work?**
A: No, use company email for all business communications. Personal email not secure or archived.

**Q: What if I accidentally sent an email to wrong person?**
A: Contact IT immediately if email contained confidential information. Email recall rarely works but can be attempted.

**Q: Can my manager read my emails?**
A: Company reserves right to access emails. Typically only accessed during investigations, legal matters, or when business need exists.

**Q: Can I use Teams for personal chats with co-workers?**
A: Brief personal conversations acceptable, but excessive personal chatting should use personal devices/accounts.

**Q: What should I do if I receive a rude or inappropriate email from colleague?**
A: Try to resolve directly if comfortable. If not resolved or if serious, contact HR.

**Q: Can I delete all my emails?**
A: No, emails subject to retention policy. Archive old emails instead of deleting. Never delete emails related to legal matters or investigations.
